# Paleontological roots

## History in a nutshell

The consortium has its roots in paleontological data analysis. Quantitative paleontology has become a mature digital discipline thanks to digital databases that allow the aggregation of data from a variety of sources, including fossil occurrences, stratigraphy, taxonomy. This discipline has its roots in biodiversity studies, with the primary questions focusing on the details how life has reached the incredible diversity that we see today.

## Researching the Coevolution of Life and Earth

When it comes to up-to-date research the traditional disciplinary boundaries are barely noticable, and the gaps between traditional disciplines are getting filled with cross-disciplinary studies. These studies necessarily utilize data that come from various traditional backgrounds, across all Earth Systems. 


Better understanding the history of how the abiotic environment influenced life in the geological past, and vice versa, is essential if we are to understand how we, humans have been and are influencing our environment. The tools utilized for this historic endeavour is frequently overlapping with the tools needed for modern-focussed studies, making the temporal separation between past and modern research focus entirely obsolete. The boundaries between macroecological, macroevolutionary and paleontological and paleoclimatological are past: we stand united in the face of how much we do not understand about the incredible complexity of Life on Earth and their interaction.


Why paleontolgy as the basis? Understanding the distribution of life on Earth today is burdened with many complexities, which is even more difficult to tackle when we try to use fossils. Getting to the simple parameters like where, when and which taxon lived on the surface of Earth is the result of a complex modelling process that is ultimately a model of Earth's geological past. 


The research community of Earth Life interaction is in deep need for a globally-integrated data system that is, openly accessible, FAIR equiteable, distributed and democratized. 

The cost of such an infrastructure is beyond what we currently can attain.
